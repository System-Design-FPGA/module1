    Assessment

    Exam
    a. The written exam will take place online. Those who attend the exam from home should provide a stable connection and good camera (webcam), those one attend in-site the exam will take place in the Laboratory.
    b. A final project will be given to the students based on the skills learnt during the course. The topic and materials will be discussed during the course. Students will be grouped in 4-5 persons, and they should provide the final project before the deadline.
    Lab Practice / Coursework
    a. The coursework includes a presentation/seminar with the specific topic for each student. The report should be written in Max. 4 pages, A4 IEEE Conference, double pages format and submit before the deadline. (MS. Word and latex format is available on the course website).
    b. There is an alternative for students to do extra Lab practice instead of seminar/presentation.
    Lecture details Tutorial Laboratory /Coursework
    The aim for this week is to be familiar with a Digital System design with FPGA hardware. The goals are: Getting started with Vivado design suite. Write a given program based on the materials learned with the hand-on tutorial.
    What are the Embedded System essentials? Why do we need FPGA? How will FPGA increase the performance/efficiency of a digital system? The following topics will be covered: There will be a hand-on tutorial to get start with design tools. we show how students can easily start programming FPGA with a simple program. a. VHDL/Verilog program that will be tested using the testbench.
    • Introduction to Heterogeneous System Architecture.
    • Overview of FPGA architecture.
    • Design flow including basic circuit synthesis, FPGA logic blocks, hard blocks, I/O mapping to FPGA blocks, placement, and routing, configuring FPGAs.
    		PS1. Extra resources are available on the website (e-learning webpage, GitHub repo, blackboard, etc.) PS2. We focus on Pynq and Zedboard during this course. b. The time diagram created by the test bench will be discussed and students should be able to interpret how the logic works.

    By the end of the module, students should be able to:

    Analyse how mathematics is performed in custom circuits, and how simple units can be combined to implement complex compute datapaths. Through the practical use of the Verilog hardware description language (HDL), consolidate understanding of the theory of combinational and sequential circuits and how these combine in the design of digital computing circuits. Evaluate the digital design flow as currently practised professionally in industry, with reference to field programmable gate arrays, and at a more general level, to custom application specific integrated circuits. Devise a testing strategy and design a testbench to evaluate the functional correctness of a circuit using the testing features of the Verilog HDL and a professional standard simulator. Reason about the performance of synchronous digital circuits, based on the timing of basic components, and how pipelining affects performance, and how this differs from measuring the performance of software running on processors.
